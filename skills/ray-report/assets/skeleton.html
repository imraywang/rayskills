First paragraph of the article — gets a drop cap on the first character. Make this paragraph concrete and immediate. Open with a scene, a number, or a striking observation. Don't open with abstractions like "Recently I have been thinking about..."

Second paragraph builds on the first. Explain what's happening and why it matters. Keep paragraphs flowing — don't break into bullets here.

Third paragraph (optional) — set up the thesis or central question that the article will address. Then a pull quote often makes sense here, especially if you have a pivotal voice to cite.
